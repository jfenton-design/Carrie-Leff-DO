Skin Health

Retinoids for Skin Care

How to start and use retinoids (vitamin A derivatives) to treat sun damage, fine wrinkles, and uneven skin tone — without irritation.

Retinoids are powerful medications. Starting slowly is essential — rushing into daily use causes redness, flaking, and peeling. Follow this guide and your skin will adapt.

Starting Schedule

Week by week approach:

  1. 1
    Weeks 1–3: Apply 1–3 times per week (every other night or every 2–3 nights). Use a calendar to stay consistent.
  2. 2
    After 3 weeks: Gradually increase toward nightly use, as tolerated.
  3. 3
    If irritation occurs: Stop for several days until your skin recovers, then restart at a lower frequency and increase even more slowly. Your skin will eventually adapt.

How to Apply

Every time you apply:

  1. 1
    Cleanse gently with CeraVe Foaming Facial Cleanser or Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser. Pat — do not rub — skin dry.
  2. 2
    Wait 15–20 minutes for skin to dry completely before applying. (Applying to damp skin increases irritation.)
  3. 3
    Apply a pea-sized amount for the entire face — less is more. Gently rub in a thin film over all areas you want to treat, avoiding the corners of the eyes and mouth.
  4. 4
    Apply to the entire treatment area — not just problem spots. The neck, chest, arms, and hands can also be treated.
  5. 5
    Apply at bedtime.

Essential Rules

Sunscreen Is Non-Negotiable

Use sunscreen containing Zinc Oxide, Titanium Dioxide, or Parsol every day. Recommended brands: Elta MD or Solar products.

If you know you'll be in intense sun (beach, midday outdoor activities), skip the retinoid the night before.

Avoid These While Starting Out

Do not use the following while your skin is adjusting — they will be too irritating:
• Astringents or toners
• Exfoliants or scrubs
• Masks
• Alpha-hydroxy acid or glycolic acid products

Your provider may add these back later as your skin adapts.

Do not use retinoids during pregnancy. Oral retinoids are known to cause birth defects. Stop your topical retinoid if you think you may be pregnant.

When Will I See Results?

Timeline

Results take at least 3–4 months to appear. After 2–4 months, some patients notice fine red scaly areas — this is normal and clears with continued use.

After 6–12 months of daily use, you may transition to applying 2–3 times per week for maintenance.
